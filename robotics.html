Mobile Crane

2019

The Challenge

MEGR 3156 is a course where students are given a design challenge and complete with other teams at the end of the semester. The received
challenge was to design a remote vehicle that could traverse an obstacle course, ascend an incline, and transport an object across an 8” moat. The dimensions were restricted to 6” x 6” x 12” at the start and end of the run, but not restricted in between. Throughout the semester, students had to present a conceptual and final design review before approval to begin manufacturing. Students had to consider cost and weight of the design in addition to physical elements such as dynamic forces, stresses, fatigue, and mechanical advantage.  

Computer Aided Design (CAD)

The robot was design using Solid Works. 3D printed parts make up most of the major components. This design choice was made because 3D printed enables rapid prototyping of complex parts with the sacrifice of durability. But with the unique configuration of the parts, ABS proved to be a strong enough material with a safety factor included in the calculations.

Design

Parts of this robot gained inspiration from many existing engineering machines. A lot of teams designed their robots to have a passive arm pivot across a gear with the claw at the end, but our approach was different. Where people wanted to stay behind the moat, we were willing to drive off it. This idea came from watching a video of how concrete bridges were assembled with massive machines. And that was not the only design inspiration. The base was build similar to how FPV drones are assembled which you can see from the double layered body. With design comes justifications and the team ended up with over forty pages of Mathcad calculations to present for our Final Design Review.

IoT Project

2018

The Story

When I used to live in an apartment complex with neighbors who were my friends, they would come visit our place every day as the typical hang out spot. Their company was always welcomed, but it soon became a burden when one of us had to get up from our rooms to unlock the door that was 30ft away. It got to the point where someone would knock on the door and neither of us would stop what we were doing to get up and unlock the door. We were that lazy. It eventually came down to having a particle photon, Arduino kit, and the remaining motivation that we had left to try and build a device that would allow the door to unlock itself.  

Simple but Brilliant

The electronic components that this beast of a machine has consists of a battery pack, particle photon, motor controller, stepper motor, and a 3D printed enclosure. The idea was simple, have an app on your phone with a single button to press that locks and unlocks the door. The code was done on Particle’s online IDE and enabled us to relay Boolean data between the device and an online GUI. In the end, it worked out great and both the people in the rooms and the people trying to come in were happy.

Design Project

2018

The University of Charlotte requires that students take MEGR 2156 which is a design course for sophomore students. In this class we had to develop a webpage that displayed five different stages of a design process, configuring strategy, concept, modules, components, and drawings. For my project, I decided to design a “Swerve Drive” which is an advanced drivetrain in robotics. A single wheel is driven by two motors. One motor drives the wheel’s rotation while the other allows the mechanism to pivot. In this class I learned how to observe designs with basic analytical models including FRDPARRC Tables, Decision matrices, and stress calculations.  

FIRST Robotics Competition

2012-2017

Origins

FRC team 5518 was founded by me and three fellow colleagues in 2014. Before the team came about, we were freshmen in high school participating in a robotics team at South East Raleigh Magnet High. During that year we were fortunate enough experience an immense amount of energy that drove our team to the top of the leaderboards at the North Carolina 2012 regionals. From there, we went onto nationals held at Missouri St. Louis and saw the bright minds of high school students from across the world. Inspiration for starting the Techno Wolves came from the passion to build and motivation to spread the excitement of innovation to a community where STEAM could positively impact students.  

The Team

Techno Wolves FRC Team 5518 is a high school student-led FIRST Robotics Competition team based in Apex, North Carolina. We are a fourth-year community-based team partnered with Wake STEM Early College High School. Techno Wolves serves as a valuable learning experience to all of our members; the team structure is based off of an existing model in the corporate world, giving members an early, competitive edge when applying for jobs and college. Additionally, our robotics program encourages students to actively promote the team by attending outreach events, participating in community service, raising funds, and reaching out to local businesses/organizations to support us.

Mission

To enrich the minds of high school students by promoting an innovative learning experience with our community – such as local businesses, schools, and mentors – via the exploration of science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics (STEAM) through the exposure of robotics and business management principles.
